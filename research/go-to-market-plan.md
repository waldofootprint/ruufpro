## 1. Competitive Landscape Summary

### The Market Gap

Nobody offers a free professional roofing website + affordable estimate widget for small contractors. The competitive map:

| Solution | Monthly Cost | What You Get | Target |
|----------|-------------|--------------|--------|
| DIY (Wix/Squarespace) | $16-33/mo | Generic website, no roofing features | Anyone |
| Contractor+ Pro | $29/mo | CRM + generic contractor website | All trades |
| **RuufPro (free tier)** | **$0** | **Professional roofing website, SEO-optimized** | **1-10 person roofers** |
| **RuufPro (paid tier)** | **$99/mo** | **Website + satellite estimate widget** | **1-10 person roofers** |
| Roofr (Essentials + Estimator) | $398/mo | CRM + estimates + proposals + widget | Mid-size roofers |
| Roofle RoofQuote PRO | $350/mo + $2K setup | Estimate widget only (no website) | Established roofers |
| GHL Agency (white-label) | $297-2,500/mo | Website + CRM + automations + ads | Mid-size roofers |
| Hook Agency | $3,000-15,000/mo | Full-service marketing | $2M+ revenue roofers |
| Roofing Webmasters | $750-5,000/mo | SEO + website + content | All roofing companies |

### Competitor Strengths & Weaknesses

**Roofle** ($350/mo + $2K setup, acquired by SalesRabbit Jan 2026)
- Strengths: Owens Corning partnership, 20+ trade shows/year, strong manufacturer network, 7,104% ROI case study
- Weaknesses: Zero organic community presence (no Reddit, no forums, no Facebook groups), anemic social media (<2K LinkedIn, <5K Facebook), no free tier, bro-ey tone alienates segments, only 45 reviews after 5+ years, no G2/Capterra presence, SalesRabbit acquisition creates uncertainty
- Marketing approach: Relationship-driven (partnerships, events, agency referrals) — NOT digital marketing

**Roofr** ($249-349/mo base + $149/mo for estimates, $65M+ raised)
- Strengths: 100+ blog posts, G2/Capterra presence (4.6-4.7/5), ABC Supply as investor AND partner, "Built by roofers" founder story, free starter plan as entry point
- Weaknesses: Pricing adds up fast ($500-800/mo fully loaded), no mobile app, weak community presence (markets AT roofers, not WITH them), generic content voice, near-zero YouTube/TikTok, per-report fees ($13-19) punish growth, VC pressure to move upmarket
- Marketing approach: Google Ads (PPC) + content/SEO + trade shows + supplier partnerships

**GHL Agency Ecosystem** (hundreds of agencies reselling at $297-2,500/mo)
- Strengths: Proven model, huge distribution through agencies, roofing is their #1 niche
- Weaknesses: Roofers don't know they're on GHL, 2/3 of roofers unhappy with agencies, high monthly retainers ($500-2,500), agency quality varies wildly
- Our angle: Every roofer burned by a GHL agency is a potential RuufPro customer

**Contractor+** ($29/mo for Pro with website)
- Strengths: Free CRM tier as lead magnet, app store distribution, good reviews
- Weaknesses: Generic (all trades), no roofing-specific features, no estimate widget, website is a feature not the product
- Validates our free-tier-to-paid model

### Where Competitors Are NOT

These are the gaps where RuufPro can dominate:

1. **Facebook groups** — Neither Roofle nor Roofr participates organically in roofing Facebook groups (25K+ members in top groups)
2. **Reddit r/roofing** — 80K+ members, zero competitor presence
3. **RoofingTalk forums** — 11K+ members, zero competitor presence
4. **YouTube** — Roofing Insights (Dmitry Lipinskiy) is hugely influential, neither competitor has a real YouTube strategy
5. **Small/new roofer segment** — Everyone is moving upmarket. Nobody is serving the roofer who just got licensed and needs a website tomorrow.
6. **The $0-$100/mo price band** — Massive gap between free (bad) and $250+/mo (good but expensive)

## 2. Positioning & Messaging

### Primary Positioning Statement

**"The free professional website that makes roofers' phones ring."**

Everything ladders up to this. Free. Professional. Phone rings. Three words a roofer understands.

### Top 5 Messages (Ranked by Strength)

| # | Message | Why It Works | Best Channel |
|---|---------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | **"Google is hiding roofers without online estimates. Don't get filtered out."** | Creates urgency with a real, verifiable threat. 1 in 5 homeowners use the filter. | Cold email, Facebook groups, blog content |
| 2 | **"What marketing agencies charge $3,000/month for, we give you for free."** | Addresses the #1 trust issue (2/3 of roofers burned by agencies). Concrete price comparison. | Cold email to roofers, Facebook groups |
| 3 | **"Your website is live in 2 minutes. No designer. No developer. No agency."** | Simplicity + speed. Respects their time. "I'm not a tech guy" = identity, not preference. | Cold email, social media, landing page |
| 4 | **"Roofle charges $350/mo for an estimate widget. Ours is $99/mo — website included."** | Direct competitor comparison. Math that's easy to do in your head. | Comparison pages, cold email to Roofle users |
| 5 | **"You're a roofer, not a web developer. Answer 3 questions and you're live."** | Empathy + simplicity. Makes them feel seen, not sold to. | Facebook groups, podcast appearances |

### Message-to-Segment Mapping

| Segment | Lead Message | Supporting Message |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| New roofers (Segment A) | #3 (Speed/simplicity) | #5 (Empathy) |
| Burned-by-agency (Segment B) | #2 (Anti-agency) | #3 (Speed/simplicity) |
| Google-filter-aware (Segment C) | #1 (Google filter) | #4 (Price comparison) |
| Marketing agencies (Segment D) | "Stop building roofing websites from scratch" | White-label partnership pitch |
| Material suppliers (Segment E) | "Give your contractors a free website — powered by your brand" | Owens Corning comparison |

### What NOT to Say

- **Don't use Roofle's bro tone.** No "Full Send," no rocket emojis, no "sell roofs in your sleep." Our audience is skeptical and practical.
- **Don't overhype the 1-in-5 stat.** It's from one source (RoofersCoffeeShop). Present it honestly: "1 in 5 homeowners are already using Google's Online Estimates filter" — sourced, not speculative.
- **Don't say "AI-powered" as the headline.** Roofers don't care about AI. They care about results. Use "satellite-powered estimates" — it's concrete and visual.
- **Don't sell the widget on the marketing page.** The entire marketing site sells the FREE signup. The widget sells itself inside the product.
- **Don't promise specific lead numbers.** "Get 10 leads a month" will backfire. "Your phone rings more" is honest.
- **Don't badmouth competitors by name in communities.** Compare on the website, but in Facebook groups and forums, be helpful — not negative.

## 3. Target Customer Segments

### Segment A: New Roofers (1-3 Years in Business)

**Who they are:** Just got their license, maybe have a truck and 1-2 crew members. Doing word-of-mouth only. No website. Maybe 3-5 Google reviews. Revenue under $500K.

**Pain points:**
- Need credibility FAST — competing against established companies with 15+ years
- Can't afford $3K/mo for an agency or $350/mo for Roofle
- Don't know where to start with online presence
- "I'm a roofer, not a marketer"

**What resonates:**
- "Your website is live in 2 minutes" — speed matters when you have roofs to fix
- "Licensed & Insured is enough" — our templates work with minimal credentials
- "Look as professional as the big guys" — legitimacy is emotional
- The free price point — zero risk

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups (asking "how do I get a website?")
- State licensing databases (newly licensed roofers)
- Cold email (new businesses registered in last 12 months)

### Segment B: Burned-by-Agency Roofers

**Who they are:** Been in business 3-10+ years. Tried an agency ($3-5K/mo), got burned. Website they paid $5K for doesn't generate leads. Disillusioned with digital marketing. Revenue $500K-$2M.

**Pain points:**
- Trust issues — "I'm done paying people who don't deliver"
- Want control — don't want to depend on an agency for their own website
- Skeptical of marketing promises
- Still need leads but don't know what actually works

**What resonates:**
- "No salesperson. No contract. No monthly retainer." — anti-everything they hate
- "You own your website" — control matters after losing it
- "Free" — zero risk after getting burned
- Honest tone — no hype, no promises, just facts

**Where to find them:**
- Facebook groups (venting about agencies)
- RoofingTalk forums (deep pain point threads)
- Cold email (roofers with websites that look agency-built but haven't been updated)

### Segment C: Google-Filter-Aware Roofers

**Who they are:** Digitally savvy, read industry news, aware of the Google Online Estimates filter (Dec 2025). Want to stay competitive. May already have a decent website. Revenue $500K-$3M.

**Pain points:**
- "I invested in SEO and now 20% of homeowners can't find me"
- Need an estimate widget but Roofle costs $6,200/year
- Want to future-proof their online presence
- Already understand the value of online pricing

**What resonates:**
- "Google is hiding roofers without online estimates" — they already know this
- "$99/mo vs. Roofle's $350/mo" — they're comparison shopping
- "Website included free" — bonus value on top of the widget they need
- Technical credibility — satellite-powered, real pricing logic

**Where to find them:**
- Searching Google for "online estimates filter," "alternative to Roofle"
- Industry webinars (Demand IQ, RoofersCoffeeShop events)
- Cold email (roofers who have websites but no estimate widget)

### Segment D: Marketing Agencies (White-Label Partners)

**Who they are:** Small-to-mid marketing agencies (1-10 people) serving roofing clients. Many use GoHighLevel. Building websites from scratch or using generic templates for each roofing client.

**Pain points:**
- Building a new website for every roofing client is time-consuming
- Clients expect a website + lead gen but the agency's real skill is ads/SEO
- GHL's roofing snapshots are generic and look templated
- Need to differentiate from other agencies offering the same GHL setup

**What resonates:**
- "Stop building roofing websites from scratch. White-label ours."
- "Focus on ads and SEO. We handle the website and estimates."
- "Your clients get a roofing-specific site with satellite estimates — branded as YOUR service"
- Volume pricing that's more profitable than building sites manually

**Where to find them:**
- GHL Skool communities (roofing niche discussions)
- Facebook groups for marketing agencies
- Cold email (agencies whose roofing clients have mediocre websites)
- Roofle/Roofr partner pages (agencies listed there might want a cheaper alternative)

**White-label pricing (to be validated):**
- Option A: Per-client fee ($49-79/mo per site) — agency marks up to client
- Option B: Flat monthly fee ($199-499/mo) for unlimited client sites
- Option C: Rev-share on widget upgrades — agency gets % of $99/mo widget revenue
- Need to test which model agencies prefer

### Segment E: Roofing Material Suppliers

**Who they are:** Regional roofing supply houses and distributors. Not ABC Supply scale (they're with Roofr) — think local/regional players with 50-500 contractor relationships.

**Pain points:**
- Competing with bigger supply houses for contractor loyalty
- Want to offer value-added services to keep contractors buying from them

**What resonates:**

**Where to find them:**

**Partnership pricing (to be validated):**

### Infrastructure: How to Send 1,000+ Emails/Day Without Getting Blacklisted

**The core formula:** 30 emails per mailbox per day. To send 1,000/day, you need ~34 mailboxes across 12-17 domains.

| Target Volume | Mailboxes Needed | Domains Needed (2-3 per) | Monthly Cost |
|---------------|-----------------|-------------------------|-------------|
| 200/day (start) | 7 | 3 | ~$80 |
| 500/day | 17 | 6-8 | ~$200 |
| 1,000/day | 34 | 12-17 | ~$350 |
| 2,000/day (full scale) | 67 | 23-34 | ~$500 |

**Domain setup:**
- Buy secondary domains — NEVER use ruufpro.com for cold email
- Variations: getruufpro.com, joinruufpro.com, tryruufpro.com, ruufpro-sites.com, helloruufpro.com, ruufpro-info.com, etc.
- 2-3 mailboxes per domain (hannah@, hello@, team@)
- Use Mailforge or Mailscale for bulk provisioning ($1-3/mailbox vs. $7/mo on Google Workspace)

**DNS records (every domain, non-negotiable):**
- SPF — authorizes sending servers
- DKIM — digitally signs messages
- DMARC — set to at least `p=none` with alignment
- Custom tracking domain (avoid shared tracking domain flags)
- Without these, Gmail/Outlook will **reject** your emails outright (not spam-filter — reject)

**Warm-up schedule:**

| Week | Emails/Inbox/Day | Total Daily (10 inboxes) | Activity |
|------|-----------------|-------------------------|---------|
| 1 | 5-10 | 50-100 | Warm-up only (automated conversations) |
| 2 | 10-20 | 100-200 | Continue warm-up, monitor inbox placement |
| 3 | 20-30 | 200-300 | Begin small cold campaigns |
| 4 | 30-40 | 300-400 | Scale campaigns, add more domains |
| 5-6 | 40-50 | 500-700 | Add second batch of domains, scale to 500/day |
| 8-10 | 50 | 1,000+ | Full production, all domains warm |

**Critical rules:**
- Keep warm-up running continuously (even during active campaigns)
- 100 emails with 0 replies = negative signal. 20 emails with 10 replies = massive positive signal
- Spread sends throughout the day (not blasted in 30 minutes)
- Monday is the best day to launch sequences. Wednesday has peak engagement. 1-3 PM recipient local time gets the most replies.

**Platform: Instantly ($30/mo Growth plan)**
- Unlimited mailboxes
- 4.2M+ warm-up network
- Built-in email verification
- A/B testing
- Auto inbox rotation
- Best price-to-value for solo founders (20 mailboxes on Instantly = $144/mo vs. Apollo $980/mo vs. Lemlist $1,100/mo)

### Email Copy: What Gets 10%+ Reply Rates

The difference between 1% and 10% reply rate:

| 1% Reply Rate Emails | 10%+ Reply Rate Emails |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| Talk about themselves ("We are the leading...") | Talk about the prospect's problem |
| Long (200+ words) | Short (50-80 words) |
| Generic ("Dear Business Owner") | Personalized opening referencing something specific |
| Salesy CTA ("Book a demo today!") | Soft CTA ("Worth a quick look?") |
| HTML with logos and images | Plain text, like a real person wrote it |
| Multiple links and CTAs | Zero or one link, zero images |

**Framework: PAS (Problem-Agitate-Solve)** — Gong.io went from 6% to 14% reply rate using this.

**Format rules (2026 deliverability):**
- Plain text ONLY (HTML = +652% bounce rate)
- No images (click-through drops 21-51%)
- No links in first email (add in follow-up 2 or 3)
- No tracking pixels (known spam trigger)
- 50-80 words per email
- One soft CTA per email
- Follow-ups in the same thread (don't break the conversation)

### Personalization at Scale (The Clay + Instantly Stack)

Manual personalization doesn't scale to 1,000/day. Here's how to personalize automatically:

**The stack:**
1. **Scrape leads** from Google Maps (Outscraper or Scrap.io)
2. **Enrich in Clay** — waterfall enrichment from 75+ providers. Gets owner name + verified email at 85-92% accuracy.
3. **AI-generate personalized first lines** in Clay using this prompt:
   ```
   Write a 1-sentence personalized opening for a cold email to {{first_name}}
   at {{company_name}}, a roofing company in {{city}} with {{rating}} stars
   and {{review_count}} Google reviews. Their website status is: {{website_status}}.
   Keep it under 20 words. Sound human. Only use the provided fields. Never invent facts.
   ```
4. **Push enriched contacts** + personalized fields to Instantly via integration
5. **Instantly runs the sequence** with mail merge variables + inbox rotation

**Personalization variables that matter most:**
- {{first_name}} — basic but essential
- {{company_name}} — in subject line = +22% open rate
- {{city}} — geographic relevance
- {{review_count}} / {{rating}} — proves you actually looked them up
- {{website_status}} — "no website" vs. "outdated site" vs. "no estimate widget" drives which sub-campaign they enter

**Variables for agencies/suppliers:**
- {{agency_name}} / {{supply_house_name}}
- {{number_of_roofing_clients}} (if findable)
- {{competitor_tool_they_use}} (if on Roofle/Roofr partner page)

### A/B Testing Protocol

Don't guess what works. Test everything on small batches, then scale winners.

**How to test:**
- Send variation A to 100 contacts, variation B to 100 contacts
- Run for 3-5 days (long enough for the full sequence to play out)
- Compare reply rates
- Declare winner when one variation has 95%+ statistical significance
- Kill the loser. Scale the winner. Test the next variable.

**What to test (in priority order):**
1. Subject lines (biggest impact on open rate)
2. Opening line (biggest impact on reply rate)
3. CTA phrasing (biggest impact on positive reply rate)
4. Email length (50 words vs. 80 words vs. 100 words)
5. Sequence timing (Day 0-3-10-17 vs. Day 0-2-7-14)

**Example subject line test:**
- A: "{{first_name}} - free roofing website for {{company_name}}"
- B: "quick question about {{company_name}}"
- C: "{{company_name}} — are you showing up on Google?"
- D: "free website for {{first_name}}"

Run each to 100 contacts. The winner becomes your default. Then test the next variable.

### Price Sensitivity Survey (for beta roofers)

Ask 5-10 beta users:
1. "At what price would the estimate widget be so cheap you'd question its quality?" → floor
2. "At what price would you consider it a great deal?" → sweet spot
3. "At what price would it start to feel expensive?" → ceiling
4. "At what price would it be too expensive to consider?" → rejection point

This is the Van Westendorp model — gives you a price range, not just a number.

## 8. Competitive Response Plan

### If Roofle or Roofr copies the free website model:

- **Our advantage:** We're already there. First-mover in the "free website + affordable widget" space.
- **Response:** Double down on community trust. "We've been giving roofers free websites since day one — not as a loss leader for a $350/mo platform."
- **Moat:** "Powered by RuufPro" network effect — every free site is a marketing asset that compounds over time.

### If competitors lower their prices:

- **Our advantage:** We can always be cheaper because our free tier IS the acquisition engine.
- **Response:** Emphasize simplicity and no contracts. Price is one factor; trust and ease of use are harder to copy.
- **Floor:** $99/mo widget is profitable. If needed, could test $79/mo. Below that, the unit economics don't work for a solo founder.

### If AI website builders get roofing-specific:

- **Our advantage:** The estimate widget. Anyone can build a website with AI — nobody else offers a satellite-powered estimate widget at $99/mo that qualifies for Google's filter.
- **Response:** Position the website as a bonus, the widget as the product. "Free website + real estimates" vs. "AI website + nothing."

### If Owens Corning or another manufacturer builds their own solution:

- **Our advantage:** We serve ALL roofers, not just certified contractors. OC's solution only works for their network.
- **Response:** Target non-OC roofers explicitly. "Not an Owens Corning contractor? You still deserve a great website."

### Long-Term Defensibility

1. **Community trust** — harder to build than software, impossible to buy
2. **"Powered by RuufPro" network** — every free site links back, compounding SEO and brand awareness
3. **Roofing-specific expertise** — templates, copy, trust signals designed for roofers, not generic contractors
4. **Price leadership** — stay the most affordable real solution in the market
5. **Agency/supplier partnerships** — distribution channels that compound over time

## 9. Brand Voice & Founder Story

### Brand Voice

**Tone:** Honest, direct, practical. Like talking to a roofer at a supply house — not like a SaaS marketing page.

**Do:**
- Use plain language ("your phone rings more" not "lead generation optimization")
- Be specific with numbers ($99/mo, 2 minutes, 1 in 5 homeowners)
- Acknowledge what we DON'T do ("We're not a CRM. We're not an agency.")
- Address skepticism head-on ("What's the catch? There isn't one. Here's how we make money.")

**Don't:**
- Use startup jargon (no "synergy," "leverage," "disrupt," "game-changer")
- Use emojis in professional copy (leave that to Roofle)
- Make promises we can't back up
- Sound corporate or impersonal

### Founder Story (Hannah's)

This needs to be written in Hannah's authentic voice, but the skeleton:

- Saw the gap: great roofers losing jobs because they didn't have a website, while mediocre roofers with good websites were winning
- Researched the market: existing solutions either cost $350+/mo or are generic garbage
- Built the thing: free professional roofing website + affordable estimate widget
- Philosophy: if the free website is genuinely great, the right roofers will want the widget as their next step
- Not an agency, not a VC-backed startup, not a roofing conglomerate — one person who saw a problem and built the solution
