# Roofing Website Copy Research

Research on what the best roofing websites say, what converts homeowners, and what's universally applicable to any residential roofer.

---

## Hero Headlines — What Works

### Patterns From Top Sites
The best headlines fall into a few categories:

**City-specific trust** (most common, strong SEO)
- "Trusted Roofing in [City]"
- "[City]'s Most Trusted Roofing Contractor"
- "Protecting [City] Homes Since [Year]"

**Benefit-driven / emotional** (higher conversion)
- "Protect Your Family and Home" — Guardian Roofing
- "Protecting What Matters Most"
- "Ensuring Your Roof Stands the Test of Time"
- "A Roof You Can Count On"

**Problem-solution** (direct, urgent)
- "Roof Damage? We're Here to Help."
- "Don't Let a Leak Become a Flood"

**Credibility-forward**
- "The Only Estimate You Will Need" — Baker's Roofing
- "[X] Years of Trusted Roofing"

### Key Principle
> "Lead with the biggest benefit you offer; the main objective is to clearly state how your service helps them and match what they're searching for." — Hook Agency

> "Ask real happy customers what they like about your company and use their language in your headlines." — Robben Media

### Universal Default Recommendation
**"Trusted Roofing in [City]"** — works for any roofer, any city, any size. It's SEO-friendly, trust-forward, and non-committal about specialty. Roofers who want something more personal can customize later.

## Service Descriptions — How to Describe Core Services

### Best Practice
> "Detailed service descriptions, short guides on spotting damage, explaining insurance claims, or comparing materials can position you as a helpful advisor rather than just another contractor." — Comrade Web Agency

> "Speak directly to your audience's concerns: timelines, warranties, safety, and insurance. Use plain language that builds trust." — CyberOptik

### Universal Service Copy (works for any residential roofer)

**Roof Replacement**
"Complete roof replacement with premium materials and expert installation. We handle everything from tear-off to cleanup, leaving you with a roof built to last."

**Roof Repair**
"Leaks, missing shingles, flashing issues — we diagnose the problem and fix it right the first time. Fast response, lasting results."

**Roof Inspections**
"Thorough inspections to assess your roof's condition. Ideal before buying or selling a home, or after severe weather."

**Gutter Installation**
"Protect your foundation with properly installed gutters and downspouts. We ensure proper drainage to prevent water damage."

### What to Avoid
- Overly technical language (homeowners don't know what "drip edge" or "ice and water shield" means)
- Vague descriptions ("We provide quality service" — says nothing)
- Walls of text — keep each service to 2-3 sentences max

## Trust / Credibility Copy

### What Converts (ranked by impact)

1. **Google reviews with star ratings** — 83% of consumers are more likely to trust a site with reputable third-party badges (Webology)
2. **"Licensed & Insured"** — table stakes, but must be visible
3. **Manufacturer certifications** — GAF Master Elite, Owens Corning Preferred, CertainTeed Select. These are the highest-value badges for roofers
4. **Years in business** — "Serving [City] Since [Year]" or "[X]+ Years Experience"
5. **BBB Accreditation** — with rating displayed
6. **"Locally Owned & Operated"** or **"Family Owned"** — strong emotional trust signal
7. **Warranty language** — "[X]-Year Workmanship Warranty" is more specific than "We stand behind our work"
8. **Financing available** — reduces friction for big purchases

### How to Display
> "Display certifications, BBB ratings, or awards that look clean or in greyscale." — Roofing Webmasters

Trust badges should be in the hero (small, subtle) AND in a dedicated "Why Choose Us" section (larger, with detail).

### Universal Copy
- "Licensed & Insured" (if applicable)
- "[X]+ Years Serving [City]"
- "Locally Owned & Operated"
- "Your satisfaction is guaranteed"

## CTA Button Text

### What Converts Best
> "Studies show that action-oriented language can boost click-through rates by up to 90%." — Creative Roofing Marketing

> "Pages with a single, focused goal can convert up to 3x higher than pages with multiple competing CTAs." — GetResponse

### Ranking (most to least effective for roofing)

1. **"Get Your Free Estimate"** — most universally effective, clear expectation
2. **"Schedule Your Free Inspection"** — slightly more specific, good for storm/insurance roofers
3. **"Get a Free Quote"** — shorter, works well on mobile
4. **"Request Your Free Roof Inspection"** — longest, most specific
5. **"Call Now"** — strong for phone-driven leads, especially mobile

### What to Avoid
- "Learn More" — too vague, doesn't drive action
- "Submit" — feels like a form, not a benefit
- "Click Here" — ancient, uninformative
- "Contact Us" — passive, no benefit stated

### Our Default
**"Get Your Free Estimate"** as primary CTA. **"Call: (XXX) XXX-XXXX"** as secondary. These work for any roofer.

## About / Value Proposition

### Pattern
Best roofing "about" sections follow this structure:
1. Who we are + where we serve
2. What makes us different (one sentence)
3. What you can expect (warranty, quality, etc.)

### Universal Template
> "[Business Name] has proudly served [City] homeowners with quality roof installations and repairs. We believe in doing the job right the first time — using premium materials, skilled crews, and standing behind every project with our workmanship warranty."

This works because:
- City-specific (SEO value)
- Quality-focused language ("premium materials," "skilled crews")
- Warranty mention (trust signal)
- No claims that need verification (doesn't say "#1" or "best")

## Review / Testimonial Framing

### Section Headlines Used by Top Sites
- "What Our Customers Say"
- "Hear From Our Homeowners"
- "Real Reviews From Real Customers"
- "Don't Take Our Word for It"
- "[X]+ Five-Star Reviews"

### Attribution Format
Most effective: **First name + last initial + city**
- "— Mike R., Denver" (feels real without full name privacy concerns)

### Our Default
**"What Our Customers Say"** as section heading. Reviews displayed as cards with star rating, quote, and "— Name, City" attribution.

## What's Universal vs. What Needs Customization

### Universal (works as smart defaults for ANY residential roofer)
- Headline: "Trusted Roofing in [City]"
- Subheadline: "Quality Roof Replacement & Repair You Can Count On"
- CTA: "Get Your Free Estimate"
- Services: Roof Replacement, Roof Repair, Roof Inspections, Gutters
- About text: The template above with [Business] and [City] placeholders
- Review section headline: "What Our Customers Say"
- Service area: "Proudly serving [City], [State] and surrounding areas"
- SEO meta: "[Business] — Roof Replacement & Repair in [City]"

### Needs per-roofer input (but has good defaults)
- Trust badges (checkboxes — licensed, insured, certifications)
- Years in business (number field)
- Phone number (required at onboarding)
- Additional service area cities (optional array)
- Custom reviews (manual entry for now, Google pull later)

### Optional customization (for roofers who want more)
- Custom headline override
- Custom about text
- Gallery/project photos
- Additional services beyond the default 4
- Logo upload
