# Roofr Competitive Marketing Analysis

**Date:** March 29, 2026
**Purpose:** Inform RuufPro's marketing strategy by understanding Roofr's positioning, acquisition channels, strengths, and weaknesses.

---

## 1. Current Pricing

Roofr overhauled pricing in March 2026 (phased rollout through May 2026). All new customers sign up under the new structure.

### Plans

| Plan | Monthly | Annual (per mo) | Key Inclusions |
|------|---------|-----------------|----------------|
| **Starter** | Free | -- | $19/report (24hr), 1 basic job board, 10 trial proposals/invoices, 5 automations, material ordering |
| **Essentials** | $249/mo | $209/mo | $13/report (2hr), 1 flexible job board, unlimited proposals/invoices, 10 automations, CC/ACH payments, SMS, e-signatures, 5 seats |
| **Scale** | $349/mo | $299/mo | $13/report (2hr), 3+ job boards, custom tags, crew management, 25+ automations, QuickBooks, role-based permissions, 10 seats |

### Add-Ons (monthly / annual)
- **Measure+ (6hr delivery):** $109/mo ($95/mo annual)
- **Measure+ (2hr delivery):** $169/mo ($145/mo annual)
- **Instant Estimator:** $149/mo ($125/mo annual)
- **Roofr Sites:** $99/mo (3 months free for pilot, originally listed at $199/mo in earlier materials)

### Per-Report Costs
- Free plan: $19/report
- Paid plans: $13/report
- Report + ESX file: $23/report
- DIY reports: $2-$4.50/image
- Expedited delivery surcharges: $5-$30

### Key Takeaway
Pricing adds up fast. A contractor on the Scale plan with Instant Estimator and 20 reports/month pays roughly $349 + $149 + $260 = **$758/month** before add-ons. This is a real pain point for small contractors.

## 2. Customer Acquisition Channels

### Primary: Google Ads (PPC)
Roofr has historically relied heavily on Google Ads for lead generation. A documented case study with Digital Shift showed:
- 55% decrease in cost per conversion
- 368% increase in conversion rate
- 2,219% increase in conversions
- Localized account structure with dynamic ad components

### Secondary: Content Marketing / SEO
- 100+ blog posts covering: roofing business tips, marketing guides, technology, materials, safety, and product updates
- Content targets both informational queries ("how to write a roofing contract") and commercial queries ("best roofing software")
- Recent posts (2026) cover AI tools for roofers, top U.S. roofing markets, and industry trends
- Blog doubles as an SEO play and a trust-builder

### Trade Shows & Events
- Attends major industry events: International Roofing Expo, Contractors Edge workshops, RISE events
- Hosts "Shingle & Mingle" branded networking events
- Presence at Petco Park (San Diego) and Soldier Field (Chicago) workshops in April 2026
- Trade shows serve as both brand awareness and direct sales pipeline

- Supplier integrations with **ABC Supply, Beacon, SRS Distribution, Roofmart** drive distribution-channel awareness
- Manufacturing partnerships with **CertainTeed, Atlas, Decra Metal Roofing** create co-marketing opportunities
- **QXO** (largest public roofing distributor in NA) strategic integration announced June 2025
- **ABC Supply** is both a supplier partner AND a Series B investor
- These partnerships give Roofr organic exposure at the point of sale (supply houses)

### Software Review Platforms
- Active presence on G2, Capterra, GetApp, Software Advice, TrustRadius
- 4.6-4.7/5 star ratings across platforms
- 100+ reviews on major sites
- These platforms drive high-intent inbound leads

### Referral / Affiliate Program
- Affiliate partners earn "competitive commissions with timely payouts"

- Partners include industry consultants and marketing agencies

### Social Media (modest presence)

## 3. Website Messaging & Positioning

### Main Headline
> "A suite of tools for roofing businesses to measure more roofs. win more deals. save more time. streamline their work."

### Value Proposition
"All-in-one roofing software with CRM, instant estimates, proposals, measurements, payments, and material ordering for roofing contractors."

### Key Messaging Pillars
1. **"Built for roofers, by roofers"** -- founder Richard Nelson is a 3rd-generation roofer (15+ years). This is their credibility anchor.
2. **All-in-one consolidation** -- replace fragmented tools with one platform
3. **Speed** -- "13 minute average reply rate" for support, 2-hour measurement delivery
4. **Social proof** -- "Rated 4.7/5 stars with over 1,000+ reviews"

### Primary CTAs
1. "Get started" (free registration)
2. "Book a call" (sales)
3. "Start for free" (no credit card)
4. "Watch demo"

### Positioning Summary
Roofr positions as the **consolidation play** -- one platform instead of five tools. They lead with operational efficiency, not cost savings. Their brand voice is professional but accessible, targeting the owner/operator who wants to modernize without complexity.

## 5. Community Presence

No evidence of significant organic community presence in:
- Roofing Facebook groups (Roofing Masters Network, Roofers Helping Roofers, etc.)
- Reddit r/roofing
- RoofingTalk forums
- RoofersCoffeeShop

Roofr writes blog content *about* engaging in these communities, but doesn't appear to be actively participating in them as a brand. Their community strategy is event-based (Shingle & Mingle) rather than forum-based.

**Assessment:** Community engagement is a weakness. The roofing community is relationship-driven, and Roofr's presence feels top-down (content marketing, ads) rather than grassroots.

- **Categories:** Technology, Running a Business, On the Job, Marketing Tips, Press Releases, Product Updates
- **Recent topics (2026):** AI tools for roofers, top U.S. markets, women in roofing

### SEO Approach
- Targeting informational keywords that contractors search for
- "How to" guides and business advice (top-of-funnel)
- Product comparison and feature pages (mid-funnel)
- Landing pages for each product feature (bottom-funnel)

### Assessment
Strong content operation. They've built a library that serves both SEO and sales enablement. However, the content is generic SaaS marketing -- it doesn't have a distinctive voice or community feel. It reads like it was written for search engines, not for roofers sitting in their trucks.

## 7. Reviews & Reputation

### Ratings
- G2: 4.6/5
- Capterra: 4.6/5 (with "Excellent" badge)
- GetApp: 4.6/5 (98 reviews)
- Roofr.com: Claims 4.7/5 with 1,000+ reviews

### Common Praise
- Fast, affordable measurement reports
- Easy-to-use proposal builder
- Good customer support
- Reasonable pricing for what you get (on lower tiers)

### Common Complaints
1. **Measurement accuracy issues** -- wrong flashing amounts, struggles with tree cover, complex rooflines, and rural properties; pitch measurement fails ~1x/month
2. **Per-report costs add up** -- at $13-$19/report, high-volume contractors feel nickel-and-dimed
3. **No mobile app** -- mobile browser experience is clunky; "the system runs best in the office, not on the roof"
4. **Limited customization** -- proposal layouts, photo placement, and disclaimers lack flexibility vs. AccuLynx/JobNimbus
5. **Weak analytics** -- no deep reporting like sales conversion by region or estimator performance
6. **No Xactimate integration** -- completely absent from insurance restoration workflows
7. **Limited production management** -- no crew scheduling, production calendar, or sub management
8. **Scaling costs** -- adding seats + add-ons + per-report fees makes the platform expensive at scale

## 9. Sales Funnel

### Entry Points
1. **Free Starter plan** -- no credit card, order your first measurement report
2. **Blog / SEO content** -- informational queries drive awareness
3. **Google Ads** -- primary paid channel
4. **Trade shows / events** -- in-person demos and networking
5. **Software review sites** -- G2, Capterra drive high-intent traffic
6. **Partner referrals** -- supplier and affiliate channels

### Conversion Path
```
Visit site --> "Start for free" or "Book a call"
     |                    |
     v                    v
Free Starter plan    Sales demo call
     |                    |
     v                    v
Try measurement      Onboarding with
reports ($19 each)   dedicated Account Manager
     |                    |
     v                    v
Upgrade to           Enterprise/Scale
Essentials ($249)    setup
     |
     v
Add Instant Estimator,
Roofr Sites, Measure+
```

### Key Funnel Observations
- **Product-led growth (PLG)** at the bottom (free plan) combined with **sales-led** at the top (demo calls, dedicated AMs)
- The free plan is genuinely useful but limited -- it creates "taste and upgrade" behavior
- No credit card required reduces friction
- Dedicated Account Managers for onboarding is smart -- roofers are not self-serve SaaS users
- Unlimited onboarding calls suggest they know their audience needs hand-holding

## 10. Weaknesses & Opportunities for RuufPro

### Where Roofr Is Dropping the Ball

1. **Pricing complexity & cost at scale**
   Small contractors face sticker shock when they add it all up. $249/mo + $149 Instant Estimator + per-report fees + Roofr Sites = $500-800/mo. RuufPro's free website + paid widget model is dramatically simpler and cheaper.

2. **No mobile app**
   Roofers live on their phones. Roofr has no native mobile app and the browser experience is poor. This is a frequently cited complaint.

3. **Weak community presence**
   Roofr markets AT roofers, not WITH them. No meaningful presence in Facebook groups, Reddit, or forums. A grassroots, community-first approach could differentiate.

4. **Generic content marketing**
   Their blog is SEO-optimized but soulless. It reads like any B2B SaaS blog. There's room for a brand that actually sounds like it's talking to roofers, not at them.

5. **Social media gaps**
   Near-zero presence on YouTube, Instagram, and TikTok. YouTube especially is where roofers hang out (Roofing Insights has 50K+ subscribers). Huge white space.

6. **Instant Estimator is an add-on, not the core**
   At $149/mo extra, the estimator widget -- the thing homeowners actually interact with -- is gated behind an add-on. RuufPro can make the widget THE product, not an afterthought.

7. **Roofr Sites is expensive and new**
   At $99-199/mo for a website, Roofr Sites is a young product (still in pilot/beta). RuufPro offering free websites with built-in estimation destroys this value prop.

8. **No insurance/restoration workflow**
   Zero Xactimate integration. Entire storm damage/insurance market is unserved. (May not be relevant to RuufPro's target, but worth noting.)

9. **Measurement accuracy issues**
   Tree cover, complex roofs, and rural properties cause errors. Contractors still need to verify on-site. Per-report model means you pay even for bad reports.

10. **They're VC-funded and must grow revenue**
    $65M+ raised, $35M revenue (2023). Series B from TCV means pressure to increase ARPU. They'll likely keep raising prices and adding features -- moving upmarket and away from small contractors. RuufPro can own the "simple and affordable" lane they're vacating.

## Direct Competitive Overlap with RuufPro

The two Roofr products that directly compete with RuufPro's value proposition:

### Instant Estimator ($149/mo add-on)
- Widget that gives homeowners instant roof estimates
- Embeddable on contractor websites
- Captures leads into Roofr CRM
- Requires Roofr subscription as a base

### Roofr Sites ($99-199/mo)
- AI-built websites for roofing contractors
- Managed hosting and updates
- Lead capture forms
- Optional Instant Estimator embed

### RuufPro's Positioning Advantage
RuufPro bundles what Roofr charges $250-400/mo for (website + estimator) into a model where the **website is free** and the **widget is the paid product**. This is a fundamentally different value prop:
- Roofr: "Pay for our platform, then pay more for a website and estimator"
- RuufPro: "Get a free website. The estimate widget makes it work."

This flips Roofr's model on its head and targets the contractors who can't justify $500+/mo for software.
