Roofle Teardown + Technical Build Plan for RoofReady

Prepared 2026-03-26 | Micro SaaS Opportunity Research

The Opportunity: Google launched an "Online Estimates" filter for roofing searches (Dec 2025) that hides roofers without online pricing. 78% of homeowners want pricing on the website. 76% of roofers don't show it. Roofle charges $350/mo + $2K setup. We offer a free website + $99/mo estimate widget.

How Roofle Works (Exact System)

What they sell: An embeddable quote widget (not a website builder). Contractors add it to their existing site. Homeowner enters address → gets instant roof price range in ~29 seconds.

Data Pipeline

  1. Homeowner enters address on contractor's website
  2. Roofle pulls roof data: total sqft, predominant slope/pitch, suggested waste factor
  3. For initial quotes, they use AI-estimated measurements (not full satellite reports)
  4. For final proposals, they integrate EagleView ($15-38/report) and GAF QuickMeasure for detailed imagery — ordered manually or auto-triggered
  5. Contractor's pre-set pricing ($/sqft by material type + markup) is applied to the measurement
  6. Homeowner sees price range across multiple material options (shingles, metal, tile, etc.)
  7. Lead is captured in Roofle's dashboard with contact info

How Roofr Works (The Other Major Competitor)

What they sell: Roof measurements + instant estimator + proposals + CRM. More full-stack than Roofle.

Pricing: $89+/mo base. Measurement reports cost $13-19/report on top. Instant estimator is an add-on. Free starter plan exists but limited.

Key difference from Roofle: Roofr owns the measurement data (they generate reports in-house). Roofle outsources to EagleView/GAF. Roofr also includes a basic CRM. But Roofr doesn't build websites either.

Main complaint: Per-report costs add up fast for high-volume roofers.

Component 1: Website Generator (Free Tier)

How Roofle does it: They don't. This is our differentiator.

Approach: Multi-tenant static site generation with Next.js

  • Use Vercel's Platforms Starter Kit (open source, built for multi-tenant apps with custom domains)
  • 3-5 roofing-specific templates (storm restoration, residential re-roof, commercial, full-service, insurance specialist)
  • Contractor signs up → picks template → enters business name OR we auto-pull from Google Business Profile API
  • Site generates in seconds at [company].roofready.com
  • Custom domain support on paid tier ($5/mo DNS via Vercel or Cloudflare)

Tech stack for generator:

  • Next.js 14+ App Router with ISR (Incremental Static Regeneration)
  • Supabase for contractor accounts, site content, lead storage
  • Vercel for hosting (handles custom domains natively, $0 for static sites)
  • Tailwind CSS for responsive templates
  • Google Business Profile API (optional) — auto-populate name, address, phone, hours, review count

Pages each site gets:

  • Home (hero + services + reviews + contact form)
  • Services (residential, commercial, repairs, gutters — toggleable)
  • About (crew photos, license #, insurance info)
  • Gallery (before/after photos, drag-and-drop upload)
  • Contact (form + embedded Google Map + click-to-call)
  • Instant Estimate page (paid tier — embeds the widget)

Cost to us per site: ~$0 on Vercel's free tier for static/ISR sites.

Component 2: Instant Estimate Widget (Paid — $99/mo tier)

How Roofle does it: Satellite imagery AI → sqft + pitch + waste → multiply by contractor pricing → show range.

Key insight: We don't need satellite imagery for V1. Roofle's precision is overkill for a "range estimate." A simplified calculator that gets within 15-20% is enough to satisfy Google's Online Estimates filter, give homeowners a price range, and capture the lead.

V1 Calculator Logic:

Input:
  - Address (for lead capture + regional pricing)
  - Home sqft (homeowner knows this, or estimate from bedrooms/stories)
  - Roof type: gable / hip / flat (dropdown with visual icons)
  - Stories: 1 / 2 / 3
  - Material preference: asphalt shingles / metal / tile

Calculation:
  roof_sqft = home_sqft × roof_factor[roof_type] × story_factor[stories]

  roof_factors = { gable: 1.3, hip: 1.4, flat: 1.05 }
  story_factors = { 1: 1.0, 2: 1.05, 3: 1.1 }

  price_low = roof_sqft × contractor_rate_low[material]
  price_high = roof_sqft × contractor_rate_high[material]

Output:
  "Estimated range: $8,500 - $12,200 for asphalt shingles"
  "Based on ~2,100 sqft roof | Prices vary by condition & access"
  [Get Your Exact Quote — Schedule Free Inspection]
  [See Financing Options]

How contractor configures it:

  • Dashboard: set $/sqft for each material (low and high)
  • Set service area (ZIP codes)
  • Customize colors to match their site
  • Embed via <script> tag or iframe (one line of code)

V2 Upgrade Path (month 2-3):

  • Add Google Solar API for actual roof area measurement
  • buildingInsights endpoint returns roof area, pitch, segments
  • ~$0.075/request, 10,000 free calls/month
  • 472M+ buildings covered, 2-5% accuracy
  • Address entry → auto-pull roof sqft → much more accurate range

V3 Upgrade Path (month 4+):

  • Partner with EagleView ($15-38/report) for detailed measurement reports on serious leads only
  • Contractor can order a full report from dashboard when lead converts to site visit

Component 3: Lead Engine (Paid — $99/mo tier)

Contact form → instant notification:

  • Lead submits form or estimate request
  • Webhook fires → Supabase stores lead → triggers notification
  • Email notification immediately
  • Dashboard shows all leads with status (new / contacted / quoted / won / lost)

Speed-to-lead auto-reply ($149/mo tier):

  • Twilio for SMS ($0.0079/message sent)
  • Lead comes in → auto-text within 60 seconds
  • Follow-up drip: Day 2, Day 5, Day 10 if no response
  • 10DLC registration required (~$15 one-time + $2/mo per campaign)
  • Cost per lead to us: ~$0.05 (3-5 texts per sequence)

Component 4: Review Automation (Paid — $99/mo tier)

  • Contractor marks job "complete" in dashboard
  • 3-7 days later, auto-text with direct Google review link
  • Dashboard tracks: reviews requested / received / avg rating
  • Cost to us: ~$0.01 per review request (1 text)

Why this matters: 35% of homeowners say reviews are #1 factor. Roofers using automated requests get 3x more reviews.

Component 5: SEO Pages ($149/mo tier)

  • Contractor sets service area (ZIP codes or city list)
  • System auto-generates: "Roof Replacement in [City], [State]" pages
  • Template: H1 + 300-word localized content + estimate widget + reviews + contact form
  • ISR regeneration keeps pages fresh
  • Schema markup for local business + service

Why this matters: This is what Scorpion charges $3K+/mo for. We auto-generate it.

How We Find Leads (GTM Playbook)

How Roofle finds leads: Trade shows, partnerships with material manufacturers (GAF, Owens Corning, CertainTeed), roofing industry publications, webinars, referrals from 15K+ users.

How we find ours (bootstrapped, $0 budget):

Week 1-2: Organic Outreach

  1. Google Maps scraping — search "roofer" in 50 metro areas. Flag every result with no website or a terrible GoDaddy site.
  2. Facebook roofing groups — join 5-10 groups. Post: "I built a free tool that generates a professional roofing website in 5 minutes — need 20 beta testers. Google just added a filter that hides roofers without online estimates."
  3. Cold DM on Facebook — message 20 roofers who have no website.
  4. RoofersCoffeeShop.com forum — post in the technology section about Google's new filter.

Week 3-4: Content + SEO

  1. Blog post: "Google's New 'Online Estimates' Filter: What Every Roofer Needs to Know"
  2. YouTube video: screen recording of generating a roofing site in 5 minutes
  3. SEO pages: "free roofing website," "roofing estimate widget," "Roofle alternative"

Month 2+: Scale

  1. "Powered by RoofReady" footer on every free site = free advertising
  2. Referral program: refer a roofer, both get 1 month free
  3. Supply house partnerships — ABC Supply, Beacon, SRS Distribution
