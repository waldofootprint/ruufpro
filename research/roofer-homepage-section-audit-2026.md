# Roofing Contractor Homepage Section Audit — April 2026

> Source: Perplexity research on top-ranking roofing contractor websites across Tampa, Houston, Dallas, Phoenix, Atlanta, Orlando, Denver, Nashville, Charlotte, Jacksonville.

## Optimal Section Order (most common top-to-bottom)

1. **Hero** (headline, CTA, phone, trust badge)
2. **Reviews / Social Proof**
3. **Services**
4. **About / Credentials / Awards**
5. **Gallery / Before-After**
6. **Contact / Final CTA**
7. Footer

## What Goes Above the Fold

- Hero headline + primary CTA ("Free Inspection" or "Free Estimate")
- Phone number visible
- One trust signal (review badge or certification)
- Concise — push quick action without scrolling
- Best sites do NOT cram multiple sections above fold

## Section-by-Section Findings

### Hero (~100% of sites)
- **Dominant CTA:** "Get Free Estimate" or "Free Inspection" (80%)
- **Phone:** Always prominently displayed
- **Background:** Real roof/job photo or house exterior (not stock)
- **Form in hero:** ~50% have it, others use CTA button
- **Trust badge:** Review count or certification badge near CTA

### Services (~95%)
- **Format:** Simple cards with icons, brief descriptions
- **Linking:** 80%+ link to individual dedicated service pages
- **Count:** 4-8 services listed
- **Top services:** Residential Roofing, Commercial Roofing, Roof Repair, Roof Replacement, Roof Maintenance, Inspections, Storm Damage, Metal Roofing
- **Position:** Below reviews, not above fold

### Reviews (~95%)
- **Dominant pattern:** Manually placed quotes with stars/badges (NOT embedded Google widget)
- **Display:** 3-5 highlighted reviews on homepage
- **Star ratings:** Always shown
- **Review count:** Shown as aggregate ("200+ reviews")
- **Link:** "See All Reviews" to dedicated reviews page
- Google widgets used by ~20%, most curate their best quotes

### About/Trust (~85%)
- **On homepage:** Yes, as a section (not just a nav link)
- **Key signals:** Years in business, GAF Master Elite (90% of top sites), license number, BBB A+, Owens Corning
- **Team photos:** On ~30% of homepages (more common on About page)

### Contact Form (~90%)
- **Position:** Standalone section near bottom + sometimes hero
- **Fields:** Name, phone, email, service type, message
- **Always:** Also a separate Contact page
- **Sticky CTA:** Top 10% have always-visible CTA button

### Service Areas (~70%)
- **Format:** City name list (most common), some with map
- **Individual pages:** ~60% link cities to dedicated landing pages
- **Position:** Lower on page

### FAQ (~70% on homepage)
- **Format:** Accordion
- **Count:** 6-12 questions
- **Topics:** Warranties, process, cost, insurance claims, timeline

### Gallery / Photos (~65%)
- **Before/after:** Very common, strong trust builder
- **Real photos >> stock photos** (top sites never use stock)
- **Position:** Mid-to-lower page

### "How It Works" / Process (~40-60%)
- **MIXED RESULTS:** First query found ~60% in Tampa/Houston, second query says "not common, more SaaS/tech"
- **Verdict:** NOT essential. Many top sites skip it entirely. When present, it's 3-5 steps.
- **What replaces it:** Reviews + "Why Choose Us" + direct CTAs. Action over explanation.

### Financing (~50%)
- Prominent in Houston (70%), less in other markets
- Usually a banner or dedicated section, not just footer mention

### Chat Widget (~40%)
- Rising trend
- Popular: Birdeye, Podium, Tidio, custom AI chatbots
- More common on commercial roofing sites

## What Top 10% Have That Average Don't
- **Always-visible sticky CTA** (phone + estimate button)
- **Award badges** prominent (not buried)
- **Before/after galleries** with real job photos
- **Numbered "Why Choose Us"** with proof points
- **Non-crammed layout** — generous spacing
- **Quick-scroll navigation**
- **Specific service sub-pages** (not just a list)

## What's NOT Common (skip or deprioritize)
- "How It Works" process section — not essential
- Video sections on homepage
- Blog/resources on homepage
- Emergency services callout (niche)
- Instagram embeds

## Key Takeaways for RuufPro Template
1. Reviews should come BEFORE services (social proof first)
2. "How It Works" is optional — consider replacing with "Why Choose Us"
3. Real photos > stock photos (but we can't control what contractors upload)
4. Sticky CTA is a premium differentiator
5. Service pages are expected — not just a card grid
6. Phone number must be impossible to miss
7. 3-5 curated reviews > embedded Google widget
