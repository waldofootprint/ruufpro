My Take

A (Command Center) looks the most "professional" and is what roofers expect when they hear "dashboard." Bento grids are trendy and give a great first impression. Best for desk use and impressing during demos.

B (Speed Dial) wins the field scenario hands-down. It's designed for the exact moment a push notification buzzes — ladder to phone call in 5 seconds. The giant call buttons and bottom tab bar are backed by hard research data. But it feels thin on desktop.

C (The Board) is proven in roofing — JobNimbus built a $100M+ business on this pattern. Roofers who used whiteboards instantly get it. Drag-and-drop is satisfying. But kanban is harder on mobile and adds implementation complexity.

D (The Feed) is the most unique option. No roofing competitor does this. The activity stream makes the business feel alive and surfaces context (speed-to-lead, revenue milestones) naturally. The right panel keeps lead details always visible.

Hot take: The strongest approach might be a hybrid — B's mobile experience (bottom tabs, giant call buttons, alert banner) with A or D's desktop layout (bento grid or feed + detail panel). Same data, completely different layouts per screen size. This is how the best apps work — Uber's driver app on phone looks nothing like their dashboard on desktop.
