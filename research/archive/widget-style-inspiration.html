01 — Containers
Card Styles
Each widget step lives inside a card. These should feel light, recede behind the content, and work on any background color.
Clean
Bordered White
Subtle 1px border, minimal shadow. Safest option — works everywhere, never clashes. Feels like a native form.
Glass
Frosted Glass
Backdrop blur with translucent fill. Premium feel, blends beautifully with hero images. Needs a non-white background to shine.
Elevated
Shadow Elevated
No border, soft shadow only. Floats above the page. Modern and clean but watch stacking with other elevated elements.
Dark
Dark Mode
Inverted card for high-contrast landing steps. Bold first impression, then transition to light for the form steps.

Recommendation
Which Combo?

Combo A (Clean Neutral) is the safest bet for an embeddable widget. It works on any contractor site regardless of their color scheme. No backdrop-filter compatibility issues. Fast to render.

Combo B (Elevated Minimal) is a slight upgrade — the pill buttons and filled inputs feel more polished. The shadow card gives it presence without being heavy. Good middle ground.

Combo C (Glass Premium) is the most visually striking but only shines on darker/image backgrounds. Could be the default for the dedicated widget section on contractor sites (where you control the background), while A or B handles the embedded/hero version.

Combo D (3D Neumorphic) is the most tactile and unique. The raised/pressed effect gives every element physical presence. Works beautifully on light grey backgrounds. Potential concern: the grey surface may feel less "clean" than pure white to some users, and it needs a grey-toned page to blend naturally.

Combo E (Glass + 3D Hybrid) takes the best of both worlds. The frosted glass container from C gives it that premium, floating-on-dark feel, while the 3D depth from D makes every interactive element feel physical — cards lift on hover and press inward when selected, inputs feel carved into glass, buttons have real weight. This is the "have your cake and eat it too" option.
