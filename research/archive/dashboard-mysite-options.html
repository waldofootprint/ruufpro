Simple Settings Panels
Stacked cards, one topic per card. The roofer scrolls through and edits what they want. No preview, no drag-and-drop — just clear, labeled fields. Like filling out a profile. Lowest complexity, easiest to build, hardest to mess up.

Option C
Section-Based Editor
Each website section is a draggable row. Toggle sections on/off, reorder them, click to edit. Gives roofers control over their page structure without overwhelming them with a full page builder. Think Notion's block-based approach.

My Take

Option C (Section Editor) is the strongest choice. It gives roofers real control (reorder, toggle, edit per-section) without the complexity of a live preview. It's mobile-friendly, scales as you add more section types, and the toggle/reorder pattern is instantly intuitive. It also naturally shows which sections are empty — nudging roofers to complete their site.

Option A is the fastest to build and would work fine for launch — but it'll feel limited quickly. Option B is the dream UX but the preview panel is a significant engineering investment and doesn't work on mobile. Option D is clean but tabs fragment the experience — roofers want to see everything at once, not hunt through tabs.

Hybrid idea: Start with C as the main view (section list with toggles + reorder), and when they click "Edit" on a section, open A-style form fields inline or in a slide-over panel. Best of both worlds — overview + focused editing.
